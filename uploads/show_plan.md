## Recommended episodes

### Episode 1 — **The Hidden Cost of Broken Continuity**

**Core question:** What if the real workflow problem is not speed, but the loss of continuity?

**Central tension:** Leaders optimize individual steps while users suffer at the handoffs between them.

**Listener transformation:** From “make the task faster” to “protect the thread between tasks.”

**Hook:** Martin likes his sequencer. That is why his frustration is so revealing.

**Why listeners would care:** This is the strongest, broadest lesson. It applies to product design, operations, research, and expert workflows.

---

### Episode 2 — **The Expert Should Not Be the Error Message**

**Core question:** Why do preventable errors keep pulling experts back into routine work?

**Central tension:** The machine knows something is wrong, but only Martin can make the workflow move again.

**Listener transformation:** From seeing expert intervention as support to seeing it as system failure.

**Hook:** A PhD student hits a sample-sheet error. The machine stops. Martin becomes the interface.

**Why listeners would care:** This episode makes “expert attention leakage” concrete and memorable.

---

### Episode 3 — **When Faster Isn’t Really About Speed**

**Core question:** Why does Martin want a four-hour run?

**Central tension:** The obvious answer is productivity; the deeper answer is memory, focus, and decision continuity.

**Listener transformation:** From measuring throughput as elapsed time to understanding throughput as preserved momentum.

**Hook:** The run finishes tomorrow, but the experiment has already gone cold in Martin’s mind.

**Why listeners would care:** It challenges a common product assumption: users do not always want speed for the reason teams think they do.

---

### Episode 4 — **The Fragile Handoff**

**Core question:** Where does a workflow become most vulnerable?

**Central tension:** Denaturing is a small step, but it carries outsized risk.

**Listener transformation:** From focusing on big technical steps to noticing small, dangerous transitions.

**Hook:** The most fragile moment in the workflow may happen before the run even begins.

**Why listeners would care:** Useful for teams designing complex workflows where small errors create costly rework.

---

### Episode 5 — **Give the Machine a Library**

**Core question:** What does Martin’s ideal future reveal about user desire?

**Central tension:** Users may not want more control; they may want the system to absorb preventable burden.

**Listener transformation:** From adding options to designing trustable protection.

**Hook:** Martin’s dream is only nine words: “Give the machine a library, and I get my sequencing data.”

**Why listeners would care:** Strong closing-episode candidate because it turns the whole case into a crisp product vision.

## Producer recommendation

Record **Episode 1: The Hidden Cost of Broken Continuity** first. It contains the master frame that makes every other episode easier to understand: Martin’s pain is not isolated friction; it is the repeated breaking of the workflow thread.
