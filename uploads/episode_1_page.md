# The Hidden Cost of Broken Continuity

## Episode Summary

Martin likes his sequencer. That is what makes this case so revealing.

At first, his workflow sounds like a familiar product story: setup friction, errors, delays, long run times, and too many interruptions. But this episode asks a sharper question: what if the real problem is not any single step, but what keeps happening between the steps?

Smart teams often optimize tasks because tasks are visible. They time them, measure them, redesign them, and improve them. But customers experience workflows as connected journeys. When those connections break, progress has to be restarted.

This episode follows Martin’s sequencing workflow to uncover a deeper pattern: the workflow is not simply slow or frustrating. It is fragmenting.

## In This Episode

- Why liking a product does not mean the workflow is working
    
- How small stops between steps create hidden costs
    
- Why users experience workflows as threads, not tasks
    
- How to distinguish task friction from continuity loss
    
- What to ask when a workflow keeps losing momentum
    

## Why This Matters

Many product and operations teams look for the hardest or slowest step. That is useful, but incomplete.

The more revealing question is: where does the work stop? Those stopping points often expose the handoffs, transitions, and context breaks that users experience most acutely.

## Where This Fits In The Series

This is the opening episode and the master lens for the series. Once you see continuity breaking, the next question is who gets pulled in to repair it — the focus of Episode 2.
