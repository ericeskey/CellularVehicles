## Executive Summary

·      **Who she is** – Catherine McIntyre, PhD, leads the **Operations & Scientific Director** role at Stanford Health Care’s Blood & Marrow Transplantation – Cellular Therapy Facility (BMT‑CTF). She oversees a regulated manufacturing facility that produces, processes and releases hundreds of cell products each year for patients with cancers, neurological disorders and diabetes[[1]](https://stanfordhealthcare.org/medical-clinics/cellular-therapy-facility.html#:~:text=Leaders%20in%20Cellular%20Therapy).

·      **Why she matters** – Her unique combination of research training (PhD in virology) and two decades of industry experience positions her as a bridge between **cell‑therapy discovery and commercialization**. She previously served as Senior Director of Global Product Stewardship & Manufacturing Support at Hitachi Chemical’s **Progenitor Cell Therapy (PCT)** division, where she led technology transfer, process monitoring and cGMP troubleshooting[[2]](https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hubfs/483397/Team%20Biographies.pdf#:~:text=Catherine%20McIntyre%2C%20PhD%20PhD%20,several%20positions%20at%20BD%20Biosciences). At Stanford she coordinates operations and scientific strategy for one of the few **FACT‑accredited** facilities on an academic medical‑center campus[[1]](https://stanfordhealthcare.org/medical-clinics/cellular-therapy-facility.html#:~:text=Leaders%20in%20Cellular%20Therapy), enabling clinical trials like the groundbreaking GD2‑CAR T‐cell therapy for diffuse midline gliomas[[3]](https://oncodaily.com/drugs/gd2-car-186269#:~:text=Catherine%20McIntyre%2C%20Aman%20Siddiqui%2C%20Nadia,Feldman%2C%20Sneha%20Ramakrishna%2C%20Crystal%20Mackall) and the institution’s first **autologous islet transplant**[[4]](https://stanfordhealthcare.org/stanford-health-care-now/2023/physicians-perform-stanford-health-cares-first-autologous-islet-transplants-in-two-patients.html#:~:text=,CT).

·      **Relevance to cell therapy** – McIntyre’s career tracks the evolution of cell therapy from early academic immunology work in the UK to commercial-scale manufacturing. She authored technical articles on technology transfer for BioProcess International and established a **cGMP‑compliant laboratory** at BD Biosciences[[5]](https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hubfs/483397/Team%20Biographies.pdf#:~:text=Before%20she%20joined%20HCATS%2C%20Catherine,several%20positions%20at%20BD%20Biosciences). Today she directs operations for a program that has delivered more than **500 CAR T‑cell infusions** and continues to expand into solid tumors and diabetes therapy[[6]](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/catherine-mcintyre-2600802_wow-500-patients-we-have-come-so-far-in-activity-6984585716215275520-O6C7#:~:text=On%20September%2013%2C%202022%2C%20Stanford,cartcelltherapy). Her perspective on scaling complex therapies within stringent regulatory frameworks will resonate with innovation‑focused audiences.

## Potential Pitfalls

·      **Overly technical deep‑dives:** Avoid going so deep into manufacturing science or quality regulations that listeners become lost; keep questions focused on decisions, processes and leadership takeaways rather than intricate assays.

·      **Confidential or proprietary details:** Steer clear of asking about sensitive trial data or undisclosed technology transfer clients; emphasize general lessons and publicly reported achievements.

·      **Patient‑specific outcomes:** Respect privacy and regulatory constraints by focusing on program‑level insights rather than individual patient stories.

·      **Dated topics:** Refrain from dwelling on past employers like BD or Novocell for too long; link them to present‑day relevance instead.

## Professional Context

- **Operational leadership at Stanford BMT‑CTF:** McIntyre oversees a FACT‑accredited, FDA‑registered facility that provides clinical cell products for therapies addressing cancer, stroke, neurological disorders and diabetes[[1]](https://stanfordhealthcare.org/medical-clinics/cellular-therapy-facility.html#:~:text=Leaders%20in%20Cellular%20Therapy). The facility coordinates collection, processing, preservation and distribution of cells for autologous and allogeneic transplant, and it ensures compliance with multiple regulators (FDA, AABB, state health departments). She directs both **day‑to‑day operations** and **scientific strategy**, coordinating technicians and quality teams to maintain “chain of identity” and product safety.
- **Contributor to breakthrough trials:** She co‑authored a 2024 Nature study on **intravenous and intracranial GD2‑CAR T cells** for diffuse midline gliomas, reflecting her facility’s involvement in complex pediatric brain‑tumor trials[[3]](https://oncodaily.com/drugs/gd2-car-186269#:~:text=Catherine%20McIntyre%2C%20Aman%20Siddiqui%2C%20Nadia,Feldman%2C%20Sneha%20Ramakrishna%2C%20Crystal%20Mackall). She was also part of the team that enabled Stanford’s **first autologous islet transplant** for chronic pancreatitis patients, providing an alternative to total pancreatectomy[[4]](https://stanfordhealthcare.org/stanford-health-care-now/2023/physicians-perform-stanford-health-cares-first-autologous-islet-transplants-in-two-patients.html#:~:text=,CT). Her name appears among authors for presentations at the 2026 **Tandem Meetings of ASTCT & CIBMTR**, indicating ongoing research engagement[[8]](https://med.stanford.edu/bmt-ct/education-training/conference-presentations/tandem-meetings-of-astct-cibmtr.html#:~:text=328%20Mature%20Outcomes%20from%20the,ALL).
- **Industry experience:** Before Stanford, McIntyre spent years at **Progenitor Cell Therapy (Hitachi Chemical)** leading technology transfer and product stewardship[[2]](https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hubfs/483397/Team%20Biographies.pdf#:~:text=Catherine%20McIntyre%2C%20PhD%20PhD%20,several%20positions%20at%20BD%20Biosciences). Her biography notes over 20 years of biotech experience in R&D, systems validation, product development and scale‑up. She has published 14 peer‑reviewed papers and holds one patent[[9]](https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hubfs/483397/Team%20Biographies.pdf#:~:text=She%20performed%20nine%20years%20of,She%20has%20also%20published%20fourteen).
- **Academic foundation:** McIntyre earned her PhD in Virology from the **University of Sheffield**. She conducted nine years of research in tumor immunology and cancer vaccine development in the UK[[10]](https://cdn2.hubspot.net/hubfs/483397/Team%20Biographies.pdf#:~:text=She%20performed%20nine%20years%20of,She%20has%20also%20published%20fourteen).
