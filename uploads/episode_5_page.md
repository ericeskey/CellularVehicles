# Give the Machine a Library

## Episode Summary

After a long conversation about sequencing workflow friction, Martin is asked what he would change with a magic wand. His answer is not a list of features.

It is a future state: “Give the machine a library, and I get my sequencing data.”

This episode asks what customer complaints are really pointing toward. Smart teams often collect pain points and convert them into feature requests. But sometimes the more valuable move is to ask what future would make those complaints disappear.

The discovery is that Martin’s frustrations are not merely isolated problems. They are obstacles between scientific intent and scientific insight.

## In This Episode

- Why complaints are not the same as the desired future
    
- How one simple phrase can reveal the larger aspiration
    
- Why customers may value protection more than control
    
- How to work backward from a future state instead of forward from pain points
    
- What it means to reduce the distance between intent and insight
    

## Why This Matters

Product teams often build around what users dislike. That can produce useful improvements, but it can also create fragmented solutions.

The deeper opportunity is to understand what the user is trying to make true. Once that future is clear, individual complaints become easier to prioritize, connect, or discard.

## Where This Fits In The Series

Episode 4 examined the fragile handoff before sequencing begins. Episode 5 closes the series by asking what all the friction was pointing toward: a workflow where the system absorbs preventable burden and the scientist can stay closer to the work that matters.

## Memorable Line

“Everything else is merely the distance between those two moments.”
