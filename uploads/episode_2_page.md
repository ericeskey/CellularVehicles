# The Expert Should Not Be the Error Message

## Episode Summary

A PhD student reaches a routine setup error. The machine stops. The student cannot recover. So Martin becomes the interface.

This episode asks a deceptively practical question: why do preventable errors keep pulling experts back into routine work? Smart teams often treat this as normal escalation. But sometimes escalation is not evidence that expertise is working. It is evidence that the system has failed to guide recovery.

The discovery is subtle but costly: the most valuable people in a workflow often become the workaround for the least valuable problems.

## In This Episode

- How routine errors quietly consume expert attention
    
- Why “expert rescue” can hide system failure
    
- The difference between expert judgment and expert recovery
    
- Why the real interface is sometimes a person, not the product
    
- What to ask when everyone depends on one indispensable expert
    

## Why This Matters

Every organization has a Martin: the person who knows how things really work, interprets cryptic failures, and keeps the workflow moving.

That person is valuable. But if their attention is repeatedly spent on predictable recovery work, the system is using expertise in the wrong place.
