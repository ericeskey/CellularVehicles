# The Fragile Handoff

## Episode Summary

The most vulnerable moment in Martin’s sequencing workflow may happen before the run even begins.

This episode asks why small transitions can create outsized risk. Smart teams often look for the biggest, most complex activity in a workflow. But Martin’s story suggests the dangerous moment may be the shift from stable library to time-sensitive material.

The discovery is that risk often spikes at boundaries. A step that looks minor from the outside can become the place where timing, materials, user attention, and consequences converge.

## In This Episode

- Why the smallest workflow step may carry the biggest risk
    
- How stable work becomes fragile during transition
    
- Why users often feel anxiety at handoffs, not during core tasks
    
- How to identify where the “risk curve” spikes
    
- Why leaders should study boundaries, not just activities
    

## Why This Matters

Organizations often focus improvement efforts on the largest visible process steps. That can miss the hidden danger zones.

If a handoff can turn stable work into fragile work, it deserves strategic attention. The practical question is not only “What takes the longest?” but “Where does the consequence of failure suddenly increase?”

## Where This Fits In The Series

Episode 3 explored speed as a way of preserving memory and momentum. Episode 4 shifts attention to the vulnerable transition before sequencing begins. Episode 5 then asks what Martin’s ideal future reveals about the workflow he actually wants.

## Memorable Line

“Pay attention to moments where stable things become fragile.”
