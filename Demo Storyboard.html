Storyboard · do not lose this

The demo, as a belief moving.

The demo is not a tour of a finished analysis. It is a short film of one belief being updated by evidence — staged as the method runs, live, on a real interview. Below is the arc, beat by beat, in the order a prospect would experience it. Tags mark what already stands in a prototype versus what is new.

Captured June 2026 · R01 Martin · source for the build
00

Open on the naive prior prototyped

On screen
The belief the market already holds, stated plainly: “just make it faster.” Nothing else yet.
Why
Name the prior out loud so the prospect feels their own assumption — then watch it move. Already the opening state of the Convergence Engine.
01

Receive the raw material new

On screen
An audio file or transcript is dropped in — the prospect’s own, or Martin’s. The thing we start from is visibly raw and real, not pre-baked.
Why
Proof the method operates on real material the prospect could hand us. This is the projection hook: “that could be my transcript.”
02

A horizontal timeline of moments prototyped

On screen
The interview becomes a left-to-right field of atoms — every moment, in the order it was spoken. Raw voice, parsed.
Why
The persistent substrate everything else is built on. Exists in the Lens Field and Convergence Engine.
03

Deep listening — the line comes alive new

On screen
Dimensions are detected and frames produced. The system listens to what is said — and what is unsaid, between the lines. A few key quotes surface. Narrative moments and peaks emerge, and the flat line of dots becomes a living graph.
Why
This is the “we hear what they didn’t say” beat — the empathy proof. The moment the prospect sees the depth of attention their own customers would receive.
04

Apply each lens — evidence builds prototyped

On screen
One reading at a time votes; voted moments rise and grow; the core ignites; divergence stays low. The timeline evolves with each lens.
New layer
At each transition to a new lens, a short teaching card: what the lens is, why it matters, and one example. The lens stops being a label and becomes understood.
Why
Accumulation is built (Convergence Engine). The teaching card on each transition is the new piece.
05

The choice-point reorganization new

On screen
Once — and only once — the timeline reorders itself: from the chronology of interview moments to the chronology of steps in getting the job done (stage the work → surface failure → recompose → protect the loop → configure the handoff).
Why
The axis flip is the structural “aha”: the same evidence, re-seen as the customer’s actual workflow. The five job stages already exist in the data (RESEARCH.stages).
The card to the right · a running record of belief moving
Raw voice→ Evidence→ Belief→ Decision-ready proposition

The right-hand panel stops being a static inspector and becomes a progress record — perhaps literally a progress bar — that fills as the demo advances. It shows the prospect exactly where they are in the move from raw voice to a proposition the team can act on. The old belief (“faster”) is visibly left behind; the new belief (pre-run readiness) forms in its place.

What already stands

  • The Convergence Engine — the naive prior (00), the timeline (02), and lens-by-lens accumulation (04) with honest votes and the prior→posterior framing.
  • The Lens Field — the persistent moment-field with lenses as overlays; the filterable cousin of the accumulation view.
  • The Reading Room — the seven readings over one corpus, with verbatim traceability.
  • The Unbroken Thread — the continuity narrative and the honest Illumina market-validation close, for the bookend.

Open questions to resolve before building this end to end

  • “Said vs. unsaid” (beat 03) needs the atoms tagged for subtext — what’s inferred between the lines vs. quoted directly. The data has observations + quotes; the “unspoken” layer would need to be marked, honestly, or it becomes a claim we can’t back.
  • The reorganization (beat 05) needs each moment mapped to a job stage. The five stages exist; the moment→stage assignment must be derived, not hand-placed — same discipline as the moment-linking.
  • The teaching cards (beat 04) risk slowing the build-up. Keep each to one sentence + one example; they should accelerate understanding, not interrupt the motion.
  • Live vs. forwarded. The “drop in a file” open (01) is powerful narrated; in the forwarded version it should auto-run with Martin’s interview pre-loaded.
