What the demo is

Job
A demo, founder-narrated, that also survives being forwarded by a champion to an exec. Its conversion job: prospect says “now I get it,” then pays to run one of their own interviews through the method.
The subject
R01 Martin — a real 56-minute interview with an NGS method-builder. One of 16 in the actual engagement; we show one, fully worked.
Martin’s sentence
“Give it a library, get back data.” Everything he’ll pay for lives in the fragile middle between those two — the expertise-hungry gap only he can hold.
Prospect’s problem
“Your team has customer evidence, but no reliable way to convert it into shared conviction about what to build, change, or stop.”
The product, one line
Dark Horse Works turns customer evidence into shared conviction — so decisions stop depending on who interprets the research loudest.

The core idea (what makes it land)

Not convergence
Continuity. Most research reports and dead-ends at the deck; Demand Discovery converts — raw voice → evidence → belief → proposition. The demo doesn’t describe this; it performs it.
The mechanic
One persistent field of real interview moments. Seven independent readings vote; voted moments rise and grow; at 4 of 7 they ignite (Hawkins: independent frames voting; Kahneman: decorrelated assessment). Divergence stays low — which makes the convergence credible.
The prior→posterior
Opens on the market’s naive prior (“just make it faster”); evidence moves belief to the non-obvious answer (pre-run readiness). Honest: votes, not fabricated probabilities.
The closing beat
The same nodes re-sort by the steps of Martin’s real job — the consensus core lands before the run executes. One continuous canvas, no page-switching. The bottleneck was never speed.
The honest close
Market validation, not attribution: the MiSeq i100 (onboard denaturation, ~4h runs, built for limited expertise) is what the category built. The proposition predicted the direction; it didn’t cause it.
